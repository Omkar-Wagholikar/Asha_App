Kumari is used in several Ayurvedic preparations. It is useful for liver disorders  and womens illnesses like pain in the periods. It is also used for dandruff and  for the growth of hair.

4. Adusa (Adhotoda Vacica) 
Adusa is small shrub grown for fencing. It is good remedy for  coughs. Use fresh 
juice or decoction of leaves. Fresh juice of leaves is used  with honey. It is useful for bleeding disorders eg. Blood  in cough, blood in stools, urine etc. 
Take 50-60 gms of adusa leaves and wash them clean.  Slow boil the leaves in one liter of water for half an hour.  About one fourth water should be left. This is adusa Kawatha for coughs.  Cool and filter it. Give 20 ml once, 2-3 times a day for three days. One can  add sugar or honey as per requirement. 

5. Kuda (Holerrhena antidysenterica) 
This is a forest tree with typical fruits. The fruit is black in color when fully  ripe. The fruit is like thin doubledrumstick but in twins. The bark or cover  of the tree-stem is used for medicine. This is a good medicine for loose  motions and dysentery. You can simply groind a piece of  bark and mix with a spoon of honey and give as  medicine. Kadha (Kawatha) also is useful. For  this take a 10 gm (2 teaspoon full) of bark  powder, add one glass (200 ml) of water and  boil. Boil it till  water remain. Cool  and bottle it. For an adult 20 ml kadha  (Kawatha) for 3 times a day is useful to  stop dysentry.
